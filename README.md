## What is the website fingerprinting attacks?

Website fingerprinting attack: a passive attack that identifies the websites a victim is visiting by analyzing Tor traffic. WF attacks exploit unique website fingerprints generated by side-channel information from encrypted connections, such as packet size, direction, and inter-packet delays, to compromise the anonymity provided by Tor.

The scenario for website fingerprinting is generally within the TOR anonymous network context. Under normal encrypted traffic, it is not of much concern; here, we specifically refer to the former.

Website fingerprinting can be classified into robustness, multi-tab, Cross-domain few-shot and early-stage based on its research questions.
